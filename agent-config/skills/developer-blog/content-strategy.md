# Phase 2: Content Strategy — Reference

**Goal:** Take a selected angle and develop it into a fully-formed content plan.

## Conversational Prompts

**You MUST ask at least two of these before producing any output.** Ask one at a time, not all at once. Stop when you have enough material.

- "What was hard about this?"
- "What surprised you?"
- "What would you tell a friend over drinks about this?"
- "What did you try first that didn't work?"
- "What do you wish you'd known before starting?"

The best blog content lives in the answers to these questions, not in the code.

## Content Type Selection

Based on the angle and conversation, recommend one of:

| Type | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Dev log** | Chronological narrative of building something |
| **Deep-dive** | Explaining an architectural pattern or technical decision |
| **How I solved X** | Problem → investigation → solution narrative |
| **Tutorial** | Teaching a transferable technique |
| **Postmortem** | What went wrong, what was learned |

## Audience Calibration

Ask who the post is for. Adjust accordingly:
- **Project users / community** — less jargon, more "why this matters to you," personality-forward
- **Fellow developers** — assumed technical knowledge, transferable patterns and decisions
- **Both** — layered structure: accessible intro and narrative, with technical depth for those who want it

## SEO and Discoverability

Suggest:
- 2-3 title options (specific, problem-oriented, search-friendly)
- A meta description (~155 chars)
- Target keywords (naturally derived from content)
- Internal linking opportunities to existing blog posts (if any)
